# Exam Prep Patterns

> Skeleton. Expand with concrete patterns after real exam-prep cycles.

## Retrieval Practice > Re-Reading
- Re-reading notes feels productive but doesn't transfer to recall.
- Active retrieval (Anki, blank-page rewrites, teaching the topic to a rubber duck) beats passive review per hour invested.

## Spacing
- New material: review at 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days, 30 days.
- Anki's default scheduling implements this — trust the algorithm unless it's actively failing.

## Concept Maps Before Cramming
- Build a one-page concept map of the course before exam week.
- Identify which concepts are foundational (used by many others) vs leaf (used by few). Spend more time on foundational.

## Past Papers
- If past papers exist, do them timed. The format-familiarity gain is significant.
- After each past paper: list what you got wrong, find the relevant concept note, add an Anki card for the gap.

## Anti-Patterns
- **Highlighting** — feels productive, doesn't help.
- **Watching lecture replays** — passive, low retrieval. Better to outline from memory then check.
- **All-nighters** — sleep before an exam beats one more hour of cramming.

Add course-specific patterns as they emerge (math vs CS vs econ have different optimal strategies).
